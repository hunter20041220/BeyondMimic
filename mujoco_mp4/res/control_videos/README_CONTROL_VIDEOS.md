# MuJoCo PD Control Videos

This directory contains the report-ready MuJoCo control-form videos generated after the earlier `rollout_videos/` trace-rendering route was found to be visually misleading.

## What These Videos Are

These videos are generated by a MuJoCo G1 model with 29 position-servo actuators. The script sets joint targets, applies a marked pelvis root-assist stabilizer, calls `mj_step`, renders the resulting physics state, and writes MP4/keyframe/metrics/summary artifacts.

The robot is held near the fixed camera center by root assist. This is intentional for presentation and is recorded in every summary JSON as `uses_root_assist_controller=true`.

## What These Videos Are Not

These are not native MuJoCo PPO, VAE, denoiser, or guided-latent policy rollouts. The existing local IsaacLab video trace files contain action statistics, not full 29-D action vectors, and the faithful MuJoCo reconstruction of the IsaacLab 160-D observation contract is still incomplete.

They are also not IsaacLab rendered videos, not official BeyondMimic Fig.5/Fig.6 evidence, and not real-robot evidence.

Each single video is 960x540, 450 frames, 30 fps, and 15 seconds. The side-by-side comparison is 1920x540, 450 frames, 30 fps, and 15 seconds.

## Guided vs Unguided

Unguided means the VAE-base target trace without test-time task guidance. Guided means the target trace after the local task-guidance objective has adjusted the latent/action trajectory. The side-by-side video compares MuJoCo PD tracking of those two target traces; it does not run native MuJoCo latent guidance.
